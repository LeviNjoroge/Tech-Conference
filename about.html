About US

Digital Inclusion are the activities necessary for the use of Information Communication Technologies by everyone irrespective of the conditional ties. The key pillars that are to be promoted through Digital Inclusion are:

  • Relevant digital skill training (skill development).
  • Adequate infrastructure and use of digital tools.
  • Affordable use of broadband connectivity.
  • Provision of quality technical support.
  • Right policy and regulation framework.

Africa Digital Inclusion Alliance is a non-profit organization that is aimed to promote these key pillars in primary schools, high schools, colleges, refugee camps, and community centers using different developmental approaches. ADIA will hold different workshops in these places which is also aimed at affiliated impact-driven initiatives. The target group has specific learning materials that have been fully designed to be used during the workshops, ADIA also provides technological devices such as laptops, tablets, and desktops to be used during the workshops. Both the instructors are also provided by ADIA during the workshops. The learning is fully psychomotor oriented in delivery of specific skill or knowledge content hence the learning is more engaging and enjoyable.

ADIA Projects and Initiatives

This is one of the most important foundational projects of the Africa Digital Inclusion Alliance as it aims to be conducted in every school across the continent. The workshops will be organized on specific topics of tech that will be mostly hands-on as the learners will have opportunity, time, and space to interact with whatever topic they are learning, even within the scope of their syllabus.

There will be a learner's activity sheet on the specific topic, the learner’s handout, instructor’s presentation slides, and an instructor's guide on the topic. For example, when the workshop is based on "Basics of Cybersecurity", there will be the "Basics of Cybersecurity Learner’s Handout", "Basics of Cybersecurity Learner’s Activity Sheet", "Basics of Cybersecurity Instructor Presentation", and "Basics of Cybersecurity Instructor Guide". Specific files will also assist in achieving the objectives of topics covered when necessary.

The learners will fill the Workshop pre-evaluation form before the workshop to track the general knowledge of the learner on that specific topic and the Workshop post-evaluation form to know the achievement from the workshops. The learner’s handout sheet, activity sheet, pre and post evaluation forms will be used by teachers and parents to know the impact of the workshop on the learners, and we will have suggestion forms printed by teachers so that ADIA can improve the workshop to be the best delivery mode depending on the target audience.

After two weeks of the training, there will be a follow-back evaluation survey form, which seeks to track the learners' progress with the knowledge.

For a good workshop to occur for every 100 learners, there should be no less than 10 laptops, charging extensions, a projector for the presentation of the slides, printed learner’s handout and activity sheet, and pre and post evaluation forms.

The workshop venue is to be made ready 30 minutes earlier before the workshop begins with the following arranged: every learner is to be given the activity sheets and power cables rightfully placed to avoid shocks and accidents. Every learner will only attend the workshops after filling the evaluation form with the assistance of the instructors and school teachers. The computers should be connected to the internet before the workshops begin for ease in operation.

For any workshop to occur, it must have 1 presenter and 3 other guide-instructors during the workshop to make it easy for the execution and practical engagement with the learning content. The school teachers might assist during the workshop process.

The workshop will take place depending on the program structure; however, a good workshop should not be less than 3 to 5 hours.

Conditions for a workshop to occur in any school:

  • More than 100 students should subscribe to the workshop by paying a specific amount of KSH.200/$2 for schools with more than 200 learners from grade 2 to grade 8. For schools with less than 100 learners, more than half should subscribe by paying the workshop fee.
  • The school must agree to open a bank savings account for the building of the computer laboratory, where 30% of the amount of money will be saved for a given period. This amount of money will be a signatory agreement between the Head administration of the school and the ADIA head office.
  • The school must agree to schedule workshops as part of their calendar of study period (term) and coordinate the prerequisite preparations for conducting the workshops.

Financial Model Strategy (Financial Distribution Policy)

  1. 50% will be sent to ADIA’s organization’s account to assist in the coordination process and other facilitations geared towards bettering the initiative. This amount will be reimbursed to the institutions for the quick equipping and building of the computer laboratory once savings have reached a specific required threshold.
  2. 30% will be driven to the savings plan, a collaborative partnership of the institution and ADIA on building the computer labs and later technical support provisions. This will ensure the sustainability of the program once it begins in any school.
  3. 20% will go to the other affiliated projects of ADIA, including the climate action strategy promoting SDG Goal 13 through local-level mechanisms and initiatives such as agro-tech to enable each child to have a laptop by the 4th grade.

Workshops will happen at least once a month in every school under the program within the term or period of learning. We are targeting 10,000 participants in the first phase of this initiative.

Technological Laboratory

This program will be implemented immediately after the institution adopts the workshops to be conducted. The computer laboratory can either involve building a new space or equipping an existing one to function as a fully operational lab.

As part of the financing plan, 30% of the savings scheme will be topped up by ADIA and its partners to build and fully equip the lab with the necessary technological tools, ensuring the integration of technology into the school curriculum.

This will provide learners with easy access to technological devices, enabling lessons to take place anytime within the school. ADIA, together with the school, will deploy trained personnel to assist in the learning process during classes.

The laboratory will also be used for other science-based and practical lessons that have not yet been established within the institution.

Key Features of a Fully Furnished Computer Laboratory

  • More than 50 computers
  • Internet connectivity
  • Surplus connectivity cables and tools
  • Technical support team
  • A board (preferably whiteboard) or projector
  • Toolkit and other infrastructural requirements
  • Tables and chairs dedicated to the laboratory
  • Printers

The laboratory will also serve to enhance accessibility to digitized learning content and provide quality technical support.

Maintenance of the laboratory will be handled by ADIA and its collaborators to ensure efficient use of the devices and tools.

Learners will also use specific websites and productivity digital tools to enhance their skills.

For areas without electricity, ADIA will adopt the Education Delivered Intelligently program, which features portable computer labs powered by solar panels. These labs are equipped with computers that have digitized learning content, just like standard computer labs.

Solar Panel Computer Labs

The solar-panel computer labs will be capable of powering the entire school, greatly improving the learning environment. These labs will operate just like any other computer lab and will serve schools facing electricity challenges.

Each solar-panel lab will include:

  • At least 21 computers or tablets
  • Sitting spaces
  • Touchscreen for projecting content
  • Power supply unit and charging ports
  • Necessary cables
  • Protection (well-fenced location)
  • Internet connectivity
  • Printers

The solar panel will be connected to other parts of the school, providing power and facilitating the learning environment. ADIA will provide the necessary tools and will also train technicians to properly maintain the computer laboratory.

Technical Support

After the installation of the computer labs and the ongoing use of ICT devices and tools essential for efficient learning, there will be a need for continuous technical support to maintain and secure the laboratory.

As ADIA, we are committed to providing free technical support to schools that have accepted our terms and conditions, which were discussed earlier. This will ensure low-cost maintenance of the computer labs, so schools are not overcharged for upkeep.

Any school with workshops three times a semester and a laboratory will receive free technical services from ADIA. They will also have 24-hour access to consultations with our technical team.

The technical support process will follow these steps:

  • The school reports the damage to ADIA.
  • The quality assurance and technical officer analyze and report the technological damages, which must be signed by the third-party teacher responsible for the laboratory.
  • ADIA office approves the release of funds for repairs, in collaboration with the school head office.
  • The budget committee provides the necessary tools for maintenance, standardized as recommended by the quality analyst.
  • Repairs are carried out.

This process will never exceed 3 days, and repairs should not take more than 1 week, especially during learning periods.

Technical support officers will file monthly progress reports on the use of the computer labs. They will ensure that the software and hardware specifications recommended by the head office are adhered to, and will provide reports on the performance of the recommended software at the stipulated times.

Broadband Connectivity

DIAG understands the importance of broadband connectivity for ensuring digital inclusion in schools and across the country. As part of our initiative, we will install Wi-Fi in schools, bringing numerous benefits to both teachers and students:

  • Allowing teachers to access relevant learning content for students.
  • Promoting ease of communication between heads of departments and administrative offices.
  • Exposing teachers to various digital learning tools that increase their productivity.
  • Allowing students to access specific websites and videos for enhanced learning.
  • Facilitating practical lessons and workshops through easy navigation to relevant information.

ADIA will connect all computer labs with broadband and collaborate with various ISPs to offer affordable connectivity solutions. This will help expand coverage to areas with low bandwidth and connectivity issues.

ADIA will work closely with policymakers, stakeholders, and advocacy groups to ensure the provision of reliable and affordable broadband connectivity, while challenging obstructive policies to digital inclusion. Through campaigns and forums, ADIA will be a voice for the less informed and marginalized communities, advocating for better internet connectivity.

Broadband connectivity will be co-funded by the institution and ADIA based on an agreed percentage. The institution will pay a monthly fee for the Wi-Fi and internet costs from the savings plan, though other payment plans are available. The infrastructural costs of internet connectivity will be covered from the institution’s savings plan, and the technical officer will ensure proper protocols are followed.

By 2030, Africa needs more than 700 data centers to have a productive digital economy. With the situation that Africa controls less than 2% of its data for a more digital inclusive society, our data infrastructure must have local ownership and funding for the need to be fully built and operational in different countries across Africa.

The first phase to be undertaken by ADIA will have to influence the following as we aim to this important project of Africa. The pipeline for Data centers building will be in the following stages:

  1. Have a full locally mobilized funding strategy for our data centers building.
  2. Recruitment requires skills and expertise necessary to have a well-equipped team that is well prepared for the project (project preparation).
  3. Production and sourcing for reliable sources of energy to keep the data centers. This will be part of project preparation.
  4. Establish regional cooperation through policy design and simulation to address the different “national interests”. This will aim at improving the regulations beyond border strategy. We will adopt a concept called AfCFTA for Data Centers initiative.
  5. Having the right and accessible digital infrastructure for establishment of data centers. This is to allow affordable connectivity.

The normal design for the Data centers will have to encompass the following:

  • On-site meeting facilities
  • Controlled access (multi-level security services, unique cabinet locks)
  • Protection and management (destruction and replacement of data)
  • Future-proof facilities to allow future access of information
  • Design strategy (3, 4, 5 tier design)
  • Cooling system design that is to give a positively pressured and dust-free environment
  • Smart detection systems
  • Video surveillance

The data centers will have its connectivity to and from the cloud ramp to its services through teleput, wireless, and fiber connectivity to even the landlocked countries in Africa, and that is why free borders for our data sovereignty is key.

ADIA will have a strategic-partner centric approach with Google, Amazon, Microsoft Azure, and Alibaba in provision of internet access and promotion of digital enterprise.

In Africa, agriculture accounts for close to 40% of its Gross Domestic Product. Linking agriculture to technology will not just make it a viable option but will make our economy more connected towards the provision of essential services and increasing food security. From the different crops planted and animals reared in different parts of the continent, there is a need for the transformation of the agricultural sector to be a major contributor to the technological sector, which should also be embraced for fruitful production to bring a more food-secure country.

At ADIA, we have come up with a campaign strategy that creates awareness and brings economic value to farmers in different sectors through technology adoption. This is one of ADIA's efforts to promote food security and make it easy to access information on the agricultural sector.

ADIA's Approach to the Agro-Computer Campaign:

  1. ADIA will establish ADIA Mixed Integrated Animal Farms that will aim at the production, rearing, and keeping of animals. These animals will be kept within the farm for use and leased to farmers. Among the animals that will be reared by ADIA will be chickens, sheep, cattle, goats, rabbits, and pigs.
  2. The rearing of animals will be in an integrated farm that is divided into different sections for different specific animals.
  3. The animals will be subjected to different treatments, and their use will be after maturity.
  4. The products obtained from the farm will be sold on behalf of the ADIA farm, and this will be used to improve the expansion of the farm and make it able to grow.
  5. ADIA will also receive animals through a buy-and-sell mechanism.
  6. The farm will also aid in the processing of its products like milk and meat, which will be exported and sold to increase the value chain of production.

One Laptop, One Animal Initiative:

ADIA will launch a program called the One Laptop, One Animal Initiative that allows learners from rural setups to receive a laptop by 4th grade. We will partner with different community groups and parent associations of different institutions.

We shall lease two animals/birds depending on the request of the parent/guardian, who shall take care of the animal to reproduce. Through this, every beneficiary will have the ability to take care of the animal with support from ADIA. To ensure the success of this program, the following actions will be taken:

  • Provide training to our beneficiaries on care and practices of keeping the animals safe and healthy.
  • With the help of extension officers, provide free monthly checkups to the animals and recommend the best treatments, even for other animals. This means that if someone adopts this program, they will receive free consultancy services about the animals. This will only occur if there are more than 30 beneficiaries within the same region at the time of making this request.
  • Collaborate with the climate agency to build dams/boreholes for areas with aridity and freely provide additive nutrients to the animals.
  • Provide security to the animals during drought-stricken periods by providing food, water, and transportation.

For example, when given two hens, one will be trained on handling and practices, and one will be provided with consultancy support. By the end of two years, we expect a rise in the number of hens. We will have an annual reward for the best keeper as a means of empowerment and appreciation. By the end of two years, we expect a farmer to have 200 new hens (which will be brought to the ADIA farm center at interval periods). Of these, only half will be for ADIA. The amount of money obtained will be used as collateral for the laptop for the learner so that by 4th grade, learners will own their laptops to be used for learning purposes.
